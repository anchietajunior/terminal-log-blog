This is the first entry in the log.

I'm a software engineer, university lecturer, and currently an AI graduate student. This blog is where these three pursuits converge — a space to think out loud about code, teaching, and the machines we're teaching to think.

## Why a blog in 2026?

In an era of threads, reels, and disposable content, a blog is a quiet act of resistance. Here, ideas have room to breathe. There's no character limit, no algorithm deciding who reads what.

> "The best writing is rewriting." — E. B. White

A published post can be revisited, corrected, expanded. It's living documentation of what I think and learn.

## What to expect

- **Software Engineering** — from the trenches of code. Architectures, design decisions, tools I use and why.
- **Teaching** — what I learn from teaching. How to explain recursion, why abstractions confuse, and what works in the classroom.
- **Artificial Intelligence** — graduate research notes. Papers I've read, experiments I've run, concepts I finally understood.
- **Systems & History** — about the machines and the people who built them.

## Why vintage?

This blog's aesthetic is inspired by the amber terminals of the '80s. It's not just nostalgia — it's a reminder that **simplicity is power**. Early systems did a lot with very little. Every byte counted.

Today, amid massive frameworks and models with billions of parameters, it's worth remembering: clarity is still the most important feature.
